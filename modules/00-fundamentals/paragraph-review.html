Paragraph Review

The Four Warning Signs

When reviewing a paragraph — your own or someone else's — look for these four problems:

  • No Topic Sentence — the paragraph has no clear claim; the reader doesn't know what it is about
  • Irrelevant Evidence — the supporting sentences don't actually prove the claim
  • Evidence Dump — the paragraph lists quotes and data but contains no analysis or explanation
  • Drift — the paragraph starts on one idea but gradually moves to a different one

A paragraph can have more than one problem at once. Your job as a reviewer is to name the problem before trying to fix it.

Spot the Drift

This paragraph starts clearly but loses focus. Click on the sentence where it drifts away from the original point.

Excessive screen time before bed significantly disrupts sleep quality in adolescents. A study published in Sleep Medicine found that teenagers who used devices within one hour of bedtime took 35% longer to fall asleep. This delay in sleep onset reduces the total hours of restorative rest they receive each night. Many teenagers also struggle with time management when it comes to completing homework assignments. Therefore, limiting device use before bed is essential for protecting adolescent cognitive development.

Research by Thomas and Collier shows improved long-term outcomes."

A. It has no Topic Sentence.
B. It is an Evidence Dump — it lists sources but never analyzes them.
C. The evidence is irrelevant to the claim.
D. The paragraph drifts to a different topic.

Review the Right Thing First

Most students review grammar first. That is the wrong order.

If a sentence makes a weak argument, fixing its grammar does not make it stronger — it just makes it a grammatically correct weak argument.

The correct order is:

  1. Content first — Does each sentence have a clear purpose? Is the evidence relevant? Is there analysis?
  2. Structure second — Does the paragraph follow a logical sequence?
  3. Language last — Grammar, vocabulary, punctuation.

This lesson focuses on level 1: content review.

What's Missing?

Access to green spaces in urban areas has a direct impact on residents' mental health. A 2021 study by the University of Exeter found that people living within 300 metres of a park reported 25% lower rates of anxiety. According to the World Health Organization, urban populations are growing at an unprecedented rate worldwide. Therefore, city planners must prioritize the integration of parks and green corridors into urban development projects.

Supply the Missing Piece

This paragraph has no Topic Sentence:

"For example, students who cook their own meals tend to make healthier choices. A 2020 study found that home-cooked meals contain 30% less sodium than restaurant food. This habit also develops budgeting and planning skills that are valuable beyond the kitchen."

Which of these would be the most effective Topic Sentence for this paragraph?

A. Cooking is something many people enjoy doing at home.
B. Learning to cook independently builds both physical health and life skills in young adults.
C. There are many different types of food students can prepare.

Before and After

Same content — one unreviewed, one corrected. Notice the three specific improvements.

There are many issues with social media. Studies show people spend a lot of time on it. According to Twenge (2017), screen time is increasing. Another study found mental health issues are rising. Social media platforms were founded in the early 2000s. This is a serious problem for society.
Fix 1 — Specific claim: Excessive social media use is directly linked to rising rates of anxiety in adolescents.
Fix 2 — Relevant, analyzed evidence: Twenge (2017) found that teens spending 5+ hours daily on devices were 66% more likely to report depression — suggesting that passive consumption, not social connection, drives the harm.
Fix 3 — Drift removed: The sentence about platform founding dates was deleted as it did not support the claim.

Writing Task Instructions

You will be given a weak paragraph to rewrite. This task requires you to identify what is wrong and produce a corrected version — not just fix grammar, but improve content, relevance, and structure. Allow approximately 10 minutes. Your writing will be tracked for academic integrity purposes.

The following paragraph has multiple problems. Rewrite it as a corrected, well-structured paragraph (100–140 words). Before writing, identify what is wrong.

"Exercise is important. Many people go to the gym. A study found exercise is good for you. According to researchers, physical activity helps. Gyms became popular in the 1980s. Exercise equipment is also widely available online. Therefore, people should exercise more."
