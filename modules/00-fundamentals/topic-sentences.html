The Remote Control

What is it?

Imagine your paragraph is a TV. The Topic Sentence is the remote control. It tells the reader exactly which "channel" (specific idea) they are going to watch.

Key Fact: It is usually the first sentence. It must be clear, specific, and avoid being overly broad.

Identifying the Components

Drag the fragments to build a professional Topic Sentence:

"Social media"
"has negatively impacted the attention span of teenagers."

Fact vs. Topic Sentence

Which of these is a more effective Topic Sentence?

A. The university was founded in 1950. (Static Fact)
B. The university's historic architecture creates an inspiring environment. (Arguable Claim)

Avoiding the "Kitchen Sink"

Identify the error: 'Modern architecture is beautiful, expensive, uses glass, and helps the environment.'

A. Clean Focus
B. Kitchen Sink Error (Too many ideas)

The "So What?" Test

I think apples are good.
Apples are the most versatile fruit for healthy cooking.

The "So What?" Test — Apply it

How would you pass the 'So What?' test for exercise?

A. Many people go to the gym every day to lose weight.
B. Regular physical activity is essential for maintaining cognitive function in elderly adults.
