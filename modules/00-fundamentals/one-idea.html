Introduction

What is it?

In academic writing, a paragraph is like a contract. When you start with a sentence, you promise the reader you will talk only about that specific topic.

The One-Point Rule means: One paragraph = One single idea.

Got it?

Does this opening sentence follow the One-Point Rule?
'Regular exercise improves heart health and also helps the local economy...'

A. Yes
B. No, it mixes Health and Economy

Got it?

Proving it

Point: "Regular exercise improves mental health."

A 2023 study found reduction in anxiety symptoms among regular runners.
Market for footwear is increasing year over year.

Quick Check

Your point is: 'Remote work increases productivity.' Which evidence fits?

A. Data: tasks finished 13% faster.
B. Survey: people missed office coffee.

Why does it matter?

Facts don't speak for themselves; you have to explain them. Keep your analysis strictly focused on the same idea.

Wrapping up

The final sentence "closes the door" on your point.

Therefore, regular activity helps maintain psychological balance.
By the way, it is also important to stay hydrated.

Practical Example

Point: Online learning offers flexibility for working students.
✔ Evidence: Students can access recorded lectures at any time of day.
✔ Closing: This adaptability makes higher education more accessible.
