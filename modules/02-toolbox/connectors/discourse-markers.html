Discourse Markers

Two Different Scales

Not all linking language operates at the same level. There is a critical distinction between a sentence connector and a discourse marker:

SENTENCE CONNECTOR

Links two ideas or clauses. Operates at the sentence level. Shows logical relationship: contrast, cause, addition.

"The results were inconclusive. However, the methodology was sound."
DISCOURSE MARKER

Organises the text itself. Operates at the paragraph or essay level. Signals topic shifts, reformulations, or the writer's stance.

"With regard to long-term sustainability, the model presents three structural weaknesses."

Discourse markers do not connect two ideas — they frame what is about to be said. Replacing a discourse marker with a sentence connector changes the meaning fundamentally.

When the Writer Speaks

A special subgroup of discourse markers signals how the writer evaluates what they are saying. These are called stance markers. They are a mark of academic sophistication when used correctly:

Admittedly — I acknowledge a weakness or limitation in my own argument.
"Admittedly, the sample size restricts the generalisability of these findings."

Undoubtedly — I signal strong confidence in the claim that follows.
"Undoubtedly, the most significant factor is the structural inequality embedded in the system."

It is worth noting — I flag something the reader might otherwise overlook.
"It is worth noting that the control group showed an unexpected improvement."

Significantly — I signal this point carries greater weight than the surrounding evidence.
"Significantly, no similar pattern was observed in the comparison cohort."

⚠️ Do not confuse significantly as a stance marker (= "importantly") with its statistical meaning (= "to a statistically significant degree"). Context determines which is intended.
