Transitions

Three Scales of Linking

Everything you have studied in this module operates at one of three scales. A complete academic essay requires all three:

SCALE 1 — Sentence connector
Links two ideas within or between sentences. however / therefore / furthermore / although
Scope: one sentence or two adjacent sentences
SCALE 2 — Discourse marker
Frames a new section, reformulates, or signals the writer's stance. with regard to / admittedly / in other words
Scope: opening of a paragraph or major shift
SCALE 3 — Full transition
A complete sentence (or two) that closes the previous argument, bridges the two ideas, and opens the next argument.
Scope: the hinge between two paragraphs

"These economic pressures have fundamentally reshaped individual behaviour. Yet the structural conditions that generate those pressures have received far less attention. It is at this structural level that the most significant interventions are required."

A single connector cannot replace a full transition. A full transition cannot replace a sentence connector. Each operates at its own level.

Rebuild the Transition

These four sentences form a complete paragraph-level transition. Put them in the correct order.

Yet the evidence reviewed so far addresses only the supply side of the equation.
The preceding analysis has established that housing supply has failed to keep pace with urban population growth across most OECD economies.
An equally significant set of pressures operates on the demand side, shaped by shifts in household formation patterns and changing expectations around tenure.
It is to these demand-side dynamics that the following section now turns.

Connector vs. Full Transition

Same movement between two arguments. One uses only a connector; the other builds a complete transition. The difference is not cosmetic — it is structural.

[End of paragraph on economic causes] However, the social consequences of the crisis have been equally severe, particularly among younger generations who entered the labour market during the recession years.
[End of paragraph on economic causes] The economic dimensions of the crisis are therefore well-documented. What remains less examined is how those economic shifts translated into lived social experience — particularly for younger generations whose entry into the labour market coincided with the deepest years of the recession. It is this social dimension that the following section addresses.

Which Transition Works?

Choose the transition that connects the two arguments most naturally and precisely.

[Previous paragraph argued that climate policy has focused too narrowly on technological solutions.]

However, behavioural change is also important and should not be ignored by policymakers. This technological focus, while necessary, leaves a critical dimension underexplored: the role of behavioural and cultural change in driving sustained emissions reductions.

[Previous paragraph established that early childhood interventions produce long-term educational gains.]

The evidence for early intervention is compelling. Yet the policy infrastructure required to deliver such programmes at scale remains underdeveloped in most national contexts. In addition, we need to also consider the policy infrastructure, which is not well developed.

[Previous paragraph examined individual motivations for entrepreneurship.]

Secondly, the structural environment also plays a role in entrepreneurship. Individual motivation, however powerful, does not operate in a vacuum. The structural conditions that enable or constrain entrepreneurial activity — access to capital, regulatory burden, market density — shape outcomes as decisively as personal disposition.

Where Is the Transition Missing?

One of the paragraph-opening sentences below makes an abrupt leap — it begins a new argument without closing the previous one or bridging the gap. Click on that sentence.

The evidence examined in the preceding paragraphs suggests that austerity policies produced measurable harm to public health outcomes across multiple European countries. This harm was not evenly distributed: communities with higher pre-existing vulnerability bore a disproportionate share of the burden, a pattern consistent with findings from earlier fiscal crises. Mental health services were also cut significantly during this period and many people were unable to access support. The concentration of harm among already-disadvantaged populations raises important questions about the political economy of austerity — specifically, whose interests such policies serve and whose they consistently sacrifice. It is this distributional dimension, rather than the aggregate fiscal data, that deserves closer scrutiny in the policy analysis that follows.

Writing Task Instructions

You will write the three transition sentences that connect the four paragraphs of a given essay outline. You are not writing the paragraphs themselves — only the hinge sentences that close, bridge, and open each argument. This is a precision task. Each transition must perform all three moves. Target length: 3 transitions of 2–3 sentences each. Allow approximately 12 minutes. Your writing will be tracked for academic integrity purposes.

Below is the outline of a four-paragraph essay. Write only the three transitions — the hinge sentences between each pair of paragraphs. Do not write the paragraphs themselves.

Essay topic: "The regulation of artificial intelligence requires international coordination, not national solutions."

¶1 — National AI regulation has produced a fragmented and inconsistent global landscape.
¶2 — This fragmentation creates opportunities for regulatory arbitrage that undermine the intended protections.
¶3 — International coordination mechanisms exist in comparable domains (nuclear, chemical weapons, financial regulation).
¶4 — The political obstacles to international AI governance are real but not insurmountable.

For each transition (¶1→¶2, ¶2→¶3, ¶3→¶4), write 2–3 sentences that:
— Close the preceding argument
— Bridge the logical gap
— Open the next argument

Use language from across the module: sentence connectors, discourse markers, and full transition structures as appropriate.
