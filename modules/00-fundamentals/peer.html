What is it?

The P.E.E.R. structure is a method for organizing paragraphs to ensure every argument is solid, supported, and connected to your thesis.

This framework is essential for building a strong Body Paragraph that guides the reader through your logic.

Deepening: Explanation

The most critical step is the Explanation. It is not enough to simply provide a quote or statistic; you must explain how that evidence proves your initial point. This is where you demonstrate your critical analysis.

Quick Check

Scenario: A student writes a paragraph with a statistic, but fails to explain what it means for the overall argument.

Question: Which part of the P.E.E.R. framework needs to be developed?

Deepening: Relevance

The final step, Relevance (or Link), connects the current paragraph back to the general argument (the thesis) or acts as a bridge to the next paragraph. It answers the question: "Why does this matter?"

Deepening: Balance

A strong P.E.E.R. paragraph usually spends about 60% of its space on explanation and relevance. The evidence is the support, but your analytical voice should be the protagonist.
