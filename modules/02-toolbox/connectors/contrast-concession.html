Contrast & Concession

Two Different Operations

These connectors look similar but do fundamentally different things in an argument:

CONTRAST

Two ideas of equal weight placed against each other. Neither side wins — you are simply showing a difference.

"Urban areas offer more job opportunities. However, rural areas provide a higher quality of life."
CONCESSION

You acknowledge something true on the opposing side, then immediately defend your own position. One side wins.

"Although urban areas offer more opportunities, rural communities consistently report greater life satisfaction."

Key test: If you remove the connector and both sentences still stand independently, it is contrast. If removing it breaks the logical movement of the argument, it is concession.

Between Sentences or Inside?

However connects two independent sentences. Although subordinates one idea to another — it lives inside a sentence structure. Read each carefully.

The intervention reduced hospital readmission rates by 31%. However Although , the overall cost to the health system increased significantly during the same period.

Although However the intervention reduced readmission rates, overall costs to the health system increased significantly.

Remote work has increased employee satisfaction scores across most sectors. Nevertheless Even though , concerns about collaboration and innovation remain widespread among senior managers.

The Grammar Trap

This is the most frequent error with concession connectors. The rule is rigid:

ALTHOUGH / EVEN THOUGH / WHILE → followed by a full clause (subject + verb)

✅ "Although the results were promising, further testing is required."
✅ "Even though costs rose, the programme was retained."
DESPITE / IN SPITE OF → followed by a noun, noun phrase, or gerund (-ing) — never a full clause

✅ "Despite the promising results, further testing is required."
✅ "Despite showing promise, the drug requires further testing."

❌ "Despite the results were promising, further testing is required."
❌ "Despite that the results were promising..."

Quick fix: After writing despite, ask yourself — is the next word a verb? If yes, convert to a gerund or switch to although.

Although or Despite?

Choose the option that is both semantically and grammatically correct.

Despite facing Although facing significant budget constraints, the research team published three peer-reviewed articles within the year.

Despite the government introduced Although the government introduced stricter regulations, carbon emissions continued to rise throughout the decade.

Despite the considerable opposition Although the considerable opposition from industry groups, the policy was passed with a strong parliamentary majority.

Rebuild the Concession

These four sentences form a correct academic concession. Put them in the right order.

Artificial intelligence presents significant potential for improving diagnostic accuracy in clinical medicine.
Nevertheless, the technology should be understood as a tool to support clinical judgement, not replace it.
Admittedly, concerns about algorithmic bias and data privacy present legitimate challenges that cannot be dismissed.
Even though these challenges are real, the evidence consistently shows that AI-assisted diagnosis outperforms unaided human assessment across multiple specialties.
