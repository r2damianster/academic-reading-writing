Cause & Effect Connectors

Two Directions, One Relationship

Every cause-effect connector points in one of two directions. Getting the direction wrong inverts the logic of your sentence entirely.

→ Introduces the EFFECT (what happened as a result):
therefore / thus / hence / consequently / as a result / for this reason

← Introduces the CAUSE (why it happened):
because / since / as / due to / owing to / as a consequence of

Test: after writing the sentence, ask "Does my connector point toward the cause or the effect?" If the answer doesn't match the direction of the sentence, swap families.

Which Direction?

Choose the connector that points in the correct logical direction.

The glacier has retreated by 40% over the past decade as a result of therefore rising average temperatures in the region.

Average temperatures in the region have risen significantly; because consequently , glacial retreat has accelerated beyond previous projections.

The clinical trial was halted due to thus unexpected adverse effects in a subset of participants.

Subtle Differences Within Each Family

Not all connectors in the same family are interchangeable. Register and grammar matter:

since / as — imply the cause is already known to the reader.
"Since the budget was cut, the project was delayed." (reader knows about the budget cut)

because — introduces a cause that may be new information.
"The project was delayed because an unexpected audit revealed compliance issues."
hence / thus — very formal; common in academic papers and legal writing.
therefore — formal and neutral; the safest choice in most academic contexts.
so — informal; avoid in academic essays.
due to / owing to — followed by a noun or noun phrase, never a full clause.
❌ "Due to the temperature rose..."
✅ "Due to the rise in temperature..."

Writing Task Instructions

You will write a paragraph explaining the causes and consequences of a given phenomenon. You must use connectors from both families — cause and effect — and demonstrate awareness of register and grammatical form. Target length: 110–140 words. Allow approximately 10 minutes. Your writing will be tracked for academic integrity purposes.

Topic: "The relationship between social media use and declining attention spans in young adults."

Write a paragraph (110–140 words) that explains both the causes and consequences of this relationship. Your paragraph must include:

— At least two cause connectors (because, since, as, due to, owing to)
— At least two effect connectors (therefore, consequently, thus, hence, as a result)
— Appropriate register throughout (no so as a connector)
— Correct grammar after due to or owing to (noun phrase only)
