Academic Word List in Context

The 570 Families That Unlock Academic Texts

The Academic Word List (AWL), developed by Coxhead (2000), consists of 570 word families that appear with high frequency across academic texts in all disciplines. Learning these words is the single highest-return vocabulary investment for academic writers.

Why the AWL matters:
— Covers approximately 10% of all words in academic texts
— Transfers across all disciplines — learn once, use everywhere
— Signals academic register immediately when used correctly
— Each family includes noun / verb / adjective / adverb forms
The 10 most frequent AWL families:
analyse / approach / area / assess / assume / authority / available / benefit / concept / consist

Each in a sentence:
"This paper analyses three approaches to urban governance across different policy areas."

The production problem: Most students recognise AWL words when reading but cannot produce them when writing. This lesson fixes that — every exercise requires active use, not passive recognition.

AWL Collocations: The Words That Travel Together

An AWL word without its collocation is only half useful. These are the high-frequency collocation patterns that appear most often in published academic writing:

conduct + research / a study / an analysis / an investigation
establish + a framework / a link / a pattern / a relationship
identify + patterns / factors / barriers / implications
address + issues / challenges / gaps / limitations
examine + evidence / the relationship / the extent to which
significant + increase / decrease / difference / association / implications
available + evidence / data / resources / literature

❌ Common errors: make a research (→ conduct) / do an analysis (→ conduct / perform) / a very significant (→ a particularly / notably significant) / available literatures (uncountable in this sense)

AWL or Informal?

Choose the option that uses AWL vocabulary correctly in academic register.

The study looked at how people get used to new working conditions after redundancy. The study examined how individuals adapt to new working conditions following involuntary job loss.

The available evidence indicates that socioeconomic factors constitute the most significant predictor of educational attainment. The evidence we have shows that money stuff is the biggest thing that affects how well kids do in school.

These findings are really important and have big implications for how we think about policy. These findings have significant implications for policy design and require careful consideration by relevant authorities.

Use the Right Form

Each blank requires a specific grammatical form of the AWL word shown in brackets. Write the correct form.

Replace the Non-Academic Word

This paragraph contains a sentence where a key word should be replaced by its AWL equivalent to achieve appropriate academic register. Click on that sentence.

The relationship between institutional design and policy effectiveness has been extensively examined in the comparative politics literature. Scholars have identified several structural factors that consistently predict successful policy implementation across different national contexts. The most important thing seems to be whether or not the implementing body has enough money and staff to do the job properly. Adequate resourcing constitutes a necessary but not sufficient condition for effective implementation; organisational capacity and political commitment are equally significant determinants. These findings have substantial implications for the design of future reform programmes in comparable institutional settings.

Build the Introduction

These four sentences form an academic introduction. Put them in the correct order so that the AWL vocabulary flows from general framing to specific focus.

Available evidence indicates that, despite significant policy investment, attainment gaps have persisted across successive cohorts.
Educational inequality constitutes one of the most significant and enduring challenges in contemporary social policy.
This paper examines the structural factors that sustain these inequalities, with particular focus on the role of institutional capacity in mediating policy outcomes.
Researchers have consistently identified socioeconomic background as the most robust predictor of academic achievement across national contexts.

Writing Task Instructions

You will write a paragraph using AWL vocabulary actively and accurately. The goal is not to insert as many AWL words as possible — it is to use them naturally in their correct grammatical forms and collocations. Target length: 110–130 words. Allow approximately 10 minutes. Your writing will be tracked for academic integrity purposes.

Write a paragraph (110–130 words) on the following topic:

"The challenges facing higher education institutions in adapting to digital transformation."

Requirements:
— Use at least 8 different AWL words from the families practised in this lesson
— Use each in its correct grammatical form (check noun/verb/adjective as required by the sentence)
— Use at least 3 AWL collocations from the patterns practised (conduct research / identify challenges / significant implications / available evidence / address issues)
— Do not force AWL words into positions where they sound unnatural — fluency matters as much as frequency
