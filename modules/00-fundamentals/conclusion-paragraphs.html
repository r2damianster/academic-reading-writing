Conclusion Paragraphs

What a Conclusion Must Do

A conclusion is not a summary — it is a rhetorical closing move. It has three parts, each with a specific job:

1. Restatement — Paraphrase the thesis in new words. Do not copy it.
2. Summary of points — Briefly recall the main arguments without repeating all the evidence.
3. Broader implication — Zoom out. Why does this argument matter beyond the essay? This can take the form of a recommendation, a prediction, or a call to action.

A conclusion never introduces new information, new evidence, or new arguments. Its only job is to close what was already opened.

Build the Conclusion

These are the three parts of a conclusion, but they are out of order. Drag them into the correct sequence.

Governments and educational institutions must therefore act urgently to integrate digital literacy programmes before the gap between connected and unconnected populations widens further.
It is clear that unequal access to technology perpetuates and deepens existing social and educational inequalities.
As demonstrated, the digital divide limits academic achievement, restricts economic opportunity, and isolates affected communities from civic participation.

The Most Common Mistake

The single most frequent error in conclusions is copying the thesis word for word. This signals to the reader — and the examiner — that you have nothing new to say about your own argument.

A restatement must use different vocabulary and a different sentence structure while preserving the original meaning. It is not about changing a few words; it is about re-expressing the idea.

Rule of thumb: if your restatement and your thesis could be placed next to each other and look identical, rewrite it.

Three Versions — One Winner

Original Thesis: "Remote work increases employee productivity, improves work-life balance, and reduces company operating costs."

"In conclusion, remote work increases employee productivity, improves work-life balance, and reduces company operating costs."
"In conclusion, allowing employees to work from home has proven to benefit individual performance and personal well-being while simultaneously reducing the financial burden on organisations."

New Information?

The essay argued that urban cycling infrastructure reduces pollution, improves health, and eases traffic congestion.

Does this conclusion introduce new information?

"In summary, investing in cycling infrastructure addresses pollution, public health, and traffic simultaneously. Furthermore, a recent study from Amsterdam suggests that cycling cities also report higher levels of citizen happiness and lower rates of urban crime."

A. Yes — the data about happiness and crime rates was never discussed in the essay.
B. No — it is a natural extension of the arguments already made.

Find the Intruder

This conclusion paragraph contains one sentence that introduces new information. Click on it.

To summarise, a diet rich in plant-based foods demonstrably improves cardiovascular health, contributes to environmental sustainability, and reduces household food expenditure. These three dimensions — personal health, ecological responsibility, and economic practicality — make plant-based eating one of the most compelling lifestyle shifts available today. It is also worth noting that several plant-based food companies went public on the stock market between 2019 and 2022, reflecting growing investor confidence in the sector. Ultimately, individuals, institutions, and policymakers alike have both the evidence and the means to make this transition a priority.

Close It Academically

Complete each conclusion opener with the most appropriate academic phrase.

___, the evidence presented demonstrates that early childhood bilingual education produces lasting cognitive and academic advantages.

___, addressing climate change requires not individual action alone, but coordinated international policy.

___, the three arguments above confirm that public investment in mental health services generates measurable returns across education, employment, and public safety.

Writing Task Instructions

You will write a complete conclusion paragraph for a given essay. You will be provided with the thesis and a summary of the three body paragraph arguments. Your conclusion must include a restatement, a brief summary, and a broader implication — and must not introduce any new information. Allow approximately 10 minutes. Your writing will be tracked for academic integrity purposes.

Thesis: "Increasing access to public libraries strengthens communities by promoting literacy, providing free resources, and creating inclusive public spaces."

Body paragraph arguments (already written):
— BP1: Libraries provide structured reading programmes that raise literacy rates, especially in low-income areas.
— BP2: Free access to books, internet, and study spaces reduces educational inequality.
— BP3: Library spaces bring together diverse community members, fostering social cohesion.

Write the conclusion paragraph (90–120 words). Include a restatement of the thesis, a brief reference to the three arguments, and a broader implication or call to action. Do not introduce new evidence.
