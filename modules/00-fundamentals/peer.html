The P.E.E.R. Framework

What is it?

The P.E.E.R. structure is a method for organizing paragraphs to ensure every argument is solid, supported, and connected to your thesis.

This framework is essential for building a strong Body Paragraph that guides the reader through your logic.

The Point

What is the primary function of the 'Point' at the start?

A. To list bibliographic sources.
B. To present the main Topic Sentence you will defend.

The Evidence

In the 'Evidence' phase, what information is most appropriate?

A. Direct quotes, statistics, or data from reliable sources.
B. Personal opinions based on general feelings.

Deepening: Explanation

The most critical step is the Explanation. It is not enough to simply provide a quote; you must explain how that evidence proves your initial point.

Quick Check

If a student provides a statistic but fails to analyze it, which part is missing?

A. Point
B. Explanation

Deepening: Relevance

The final step, Relevance (Link), connects the paragraph back to the thesis or acts as a bridge. It answers: "Why does this matter?"

Deepening: Balance

A strong P.E.E.R. paragraph prioritizes your voice over the sources.

60% Explanation & Relevance / 40% Evidence.
80% Quotes / 20% Own analysis.

Mastered?

What is the risk of omitting the 'R' (Relevance) at the end?

A. The teacher might think the quote is fake.
B. The paragraph will look like a list of isolated facts without a connection to the thesis.
