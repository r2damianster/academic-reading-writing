Introduction

What is it?

In academic writing, a paragraph is like a contract. When you start with a sentence, you promise the reader you will talk only about that specific topic.

The One-Point Rule means: One paragraph = One single idea.

Got it?

Q1: Does this opening sentence follow the One-Point Rule?

"Regular exercise improves heart health and also helps the local economy by creating jobs in gyms."

Got it?

Q2: Which of these is a "One-Point" sentence?

Proving it

Point: "Regular exercise improves mental health."

Correct Evidence

"A 2023 study found that 30 minutes of daily walking reduced anxiety symptoms by 20%."

Incorrect Evidence

"Furthermore, the global market for athletic footwear is expected to reach billions by 2030."

Quick Check

Q3: Your point is: "Remote work increases employee productivity." Which fits?

Why does it matter?

Facts don't speak for themselves; you have to explain them. Keep your analysis strictly focused on the same idea.

Wrapping up

The final sentence "closes the door" on your point.

Correct Wrapping Up

"Therefore, staying active is a powerful tool for maintaining emotional balance."

Incorrect Wrapping Up

"In addition, it is very important to stay hydrated while you exercise."

Practical Example

Let's see the full "contract" in action:

Point: Online learning offers flexibility for working students.

✔ Correct Evidence: Students can access lectures at any time, allowing them to keep full-time jobs.

✔ Correct Cierre: This adaptability makes higher education more accessible to the modern workforce.

✘ Incorrect: "By the way, many students also prefer using laptops over tablets." (This breaks the one-point rule).
