Adding Information

Six Ways to Add Information

These connectors all add a new point to support your argument — but they are not interchangeable. Register and position matter:

  • Furthermore — formal. Adds a stronger or more important point. Usually at the start of a sentence.
  • Moreover — formal. Similar to furthermore, but often signals the most significant addition.
  • In addition — neutral-formal. Safe in most academic contexts. Can open a sentence or clause.
  • Besides — semi-formal. Slightly informal; use with care in academic writing.
  • Also — semi-formal. Flexible position (start, mid-sentence), but overused. Avoid repeating it.
  • What is more — formal. Emphasises that the new point is even stronger than the previous one.

None of these ever appear at the end of a sentence.

Position Matters

Where you place these connectors changes the rhythm and emphasis of your sentence. Most can appear in two positions:

Sentence-initial (most common):
"Furthermore, the data confirms a significant reduction in emissions."

Mid-sentence after semicolon:
"The data is conclusive; moreover, it has been replicated in three independent studies."

❌ Never at the end:
"The results were positive, furthermore." ← Incorrect

Also is the most flexible — it can go before the main verb: "The study also confirmed..." — but this flexibility makes it easy to overuse.

Choose the Right Register

Read the full sentence and choose the connector that fits the register of the text.

The programme reduced dropout rates by 18%. Furthermore Also , it improved average grades across all participating schools.

The new policy is cost-effective. Moreover In addition , it has received cross-party support in parliament.

Renewable energy reduces carbon emissions. What is more Besides , recent data shows it now generates more jobs than fossil fuel industries.

Variety vs. Repetition

Same argument. One version overuses a single connector; the other demonstrates academic range.

Physical activity benefits cardiovascular health. Also, it reduces stress levels significantly. Also, people who exercise regularly report better sleep quality. Also, regular movement has been linked to improved concentration in academic and professional settings. Also, it strengthens the immune system over time.
Physical activity benefits cardiovascular health. In addition, it reduces stress levels significantly. Furthermore, people who exercise regularly report better sleep quality. What is more, regular movement has been linked to improved concentration in academic and professional settings; moreover, it strengthens the immune system over time.
