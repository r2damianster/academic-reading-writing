Introduction

What is it?

In academic writing, a paragraph is like a contract. When you start with a sentence, you promise the reader you will talk only about that specific topic.

The One-Point Rule means: One paragraph = One single idea. If you start a new idea, you start a new paragraph.

Got it?

Q1: Does this opening sentence follow the One-Point Rule?

"Regular exercise improves heart health and also helps the local economy by creating jobs in gyms."

Proving it

Once you make a promise (your main point), you need proof. This is the data, the quote, or the fact that supports your claim.

But watch out: your proof must support only that specific point. Don't bring extra information that doesn't belong here.

Quick Check

Q3: Your point is: "Remote work increases employee productivity." Which evidence fits?

Why does it matter?

Facts don't speak for themselves; you have to explain them. You tell the reader: "This evidence proves my point because...".

Keep your analysis strictly focused on the same idea you started with.

Wrapping up

This is your exit strategy. It's the final sentence that reminds the reader why this whole paragraph was important for your main argument. It closes the door tightly.
